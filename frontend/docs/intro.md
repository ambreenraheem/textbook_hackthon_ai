# Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics Textbook

Welcome to the comprehensive educational resource for Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics. This textbook bridges theory and practice, guiding you from foundational concepts to building real-world robotic systems.

## üìö What You'll Learn

This textbook covers the complete spectrum of Physical AI and humanoid robotics:

- **Foundations**: Mathematical and physical principles underlying robotic systems
- **ROS 2**: Modern robotics middleware and communication patterns
- **Simulation**: Virtual environments for testing and development
- **Perception**: Computer vision and sensor integration
- **AI & Machine Learning**: From fundamentals to large language models for robots
- **Motion & Control**: Locomotion, manipulation, and whole-body control
- **Human-Robot Interaction**: Communication modalities and teleoperation
- **Deployment**: Real-world applications in industry and service domains
- **Advanced Topics**: Embodied AI, multi-robot systems, safety, and ethics

## üéØ Who This Is For

- **Students** learning robotics, AI, and computer science
- **Educators** teaching Physical AI and robotics courses
- **Researchers** exploring embodied intelligence and human-robot interaction
- **Engineers** building robotic systems with ROS 2
- **Enthusiasts** passionate about the future of physical AI

## üìñ Textbook Structure

### Part I: Foundations of Physical AI & Robotics
Introduction to Physical AI, humanoid robotics, mathematical fundamentals, and physics principles.

### Part II: ROS 2 Fundamentals
Master the Robot Operating System 2 (ROS 2), including core concepts, communication patterns, and humanoid control.

### Part III: Simulation & Visualization
Learn industry-standard simulation tools: Gazebo, Unity, and NVIDIA Isaac Sim.

### Part IV: Perception & Sensing
Computer vision, sensor integration, and human detection for robotic systems.

### Part V: AI & Machine Learning
AI fundamentals, reinforcement learning, large language models, and vision-language-action (VLA) models for robots.

### Part VI: Motion & Control
Locomotion strategies, manipulation techniques, and whole-body control for humanoid robots.

### Part VII: Human-Robot Interaction
Fundamentals of HRI, interaction modalities, and teleoperation systems.

### Part VIII: Deployment & Applications
Industrial robotics, service and domestic applications, hardware electronics, and production systems.

### Part IX: Advanced Topics
Embodied AI, multi-robot systems, safety and ethics, and the future of Physical AI.

### Part X: Hands-On Projects
Five comprehensive projects applying concepts from multiple chapters:

1. **Basic ROS 2 Robot**: Build and simulate a mobile robot
2. **Humanoid Simulation**: Create a humanoid model in simulation
3. **Conversational Robot**: Integrate LLMs for natural interaction
4. **Vision-Based Manipulation**: Combine perception and control
5. **Complete Humanoid System**: Full-stack humanoid robot implementation

### Appendices
Quick reference materials including ROS 2 commands, Python/C++ guides, Linux essentials, hardware comparisons, datasets, industry standards, and troubleshooting.

## üöÄ Getting Started

### Prerequisites
- Basic programming knowledge (Python and/or C++)
- Fundamental understanding of linear algebra and calculus
- Familiarity with Linux command line (helpful but not required)
- Curiosity about robotics and AI!

### How to Use This Textbook
1. **Sequential Learning**: Progress through parts in order for a comprehensive education
2. **Topic-Based**: Jump to specific chapters aligned with your interests
3. **Project-Driven**: Start with projects and reference chapters as needed
